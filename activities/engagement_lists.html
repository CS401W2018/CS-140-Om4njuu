What are special entity characters?

Special entity characters in HTML are characters that can't be easily typed into a keyboard or may cause a browser to display incorrectly. They are typed in a sequences of characters that begin with an ampersand (&) and end with a semicolon (;).

For example:

  • < (greater than) type as &lt;
  • > (less than) type as &gt;
  • & (ampersand) type as &amp;

What are phrase tags?

A phrase tag can be used inside another tag to change the style of the text and it also used to tell the structural meaning of a block of text.

What is the difference between OL and UL Tags?

OL is ordered list which when we make a list, it will be either ordered in numerical order or alphabetical order.

Example:

  1. First
  2. Second
  3. Third

UL is for unordered list which when we make a list, it will be bulleted.

Example:

  • First
  • Second
  • Third
